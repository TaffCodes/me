Project Overview

This web application allows for people living in knit areas such as university campuses or towns to report misplaced items with image capturing. Reports of either a lost item or a recovered item are run against available records and if a match is found, the owner is notified and the recovery process begin.

Problem Statement

The current available tools for reporting lost and found items do not capture the images of the said items thus making traceability harder as word for word descriptions may not accurately describe an item.

Solution

The platform provides a secure channel for users to report misplaced items with images to facilitate accurate descriptions. Computer vision capabilities allows for automatic matching of all reports and immediate notification to owners regarding a potential match. The owner can then trace the deposited location of the item.

Design Process

Empathize

Conducted focus group discussions with students to gather insights into challenges around lost items recovery.

Define

Defined the need for submitting images in reporting tools to accurately describe items.

Ideate

Explored the use of computer vision to match and rank similarity while maintaining accuracy.

Prototype

A simple & clean user interface for submitting lost and found items with the ability to check for similarities.

Test

Adjusted the similarity scores to reflect the true capability of the matching algorithm.

Expected Impact

Increase recovery and reduce unclaimed items from reporting centres.

Notify users in real-time of a recovered item after they have made a report.

Allow for record keeping for all items to ensure trust in the recovery process.
